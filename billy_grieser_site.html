03 — About
The short version

I'm a finance professor who thinks most empirical work in the field doesn't take networks seriously enough. My research uses spatial econometrics to study how firms are connected — through supply chains, geography, shared institutional investors — and how those connections shape financial policies, investment decisions, and information flow.

Before Oklahoma State, I was at TCU and USC. I teach Financial Markets & Institutions and empirical research methods. I believe in teaching through games and experiential learning rather than slides, because finance is a contact sport.

Outside of research, I write country music, think about what it means to formalize love mathematically, and argue with LLMs about identification strategies in working papers.
